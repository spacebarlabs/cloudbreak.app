Something happened to the software we use every day.

We used to buy software. Whether it was on your desktop or a mobile device, it felt like you were in charge. And you were. The software did what you told it to do, and it kept to itself.

Today, most software you use runs in the cloud. Often, it doesn't cost anything, but it's far from free. It's actively watching you. It's not owned, but rented with your data and attention. You're the product, a product sold to advertisers.

Using modern apps enters you into an agreement with a hidden surveillance clause that most people avoid thinking about. You use these apps every day while the platform quietly harvests your habits, your inputs, and your attention. And since you didn't pay for it and you don't own it, your favorite tools might disappear when they cease to be profitable.

Community-built, open-source software is the escape hatch, but its adoption has stagnated. It just isn't easy enough to get started. Often, taking back control has meant becoming a part-time system administrator or software engineer. Setting up servers, managing databases, and running security updates is just not something the average person wants to do. You shouldn't have to learn command-line interfaces just to use a decent app without being tracked. And it's tedious and time-consuming for techies too. Wouldn't it be great to get help?

Once upon a time, you controlled the tools you depended on, and your digital life was your own private business. We think it's time for that again.
